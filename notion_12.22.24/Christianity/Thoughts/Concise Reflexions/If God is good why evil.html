If God is good why evil?

As days pass by, the continuity of time seems to unfold the character of God more every day -Or at least we think so. The term, God, has now become a synonym of pain, suffering, injustice and any type of throe. And as we ourselves experience the sting of hurt the question emerges; why does God...? This question, however, is not a recent one, humanity has battled with this puzzling issue as early as the days of Job, a man of very elevated integrity (Job 10:2-3). Although this dilemma has proven constant throughout the centuries we need to be very careful and consider the ramifications that asking this question will entail. For we are not only questioning the goodness And justice of God but we are also shaking off every gram of responsibility from upon our shoulders. Let us go back to the origins of that which defines good and evil. A moral law, Or I should say, the Moral Law (Exodus 20:1-17). Devoted Obedience to the tremendous level of morality contained in this 10 commandments would imply the immediate eradication of all evil and suffering from earth. From the savagest famines to the bloodiest wars. Jesus set these 10 regulations in a more comprehensible plane, Love God and love your neighbor as yourself (Matthew 22: 36-40). The simplicity of this statement leaves not the smallest seat available for confusion. It is not necessary to climb the lather of Abstract to obtain an explanation of what to love our neighbour as we love ourselves mean but let us make a few contrast in what I think, the most debated arenas. Global suffering, familiar contort and internal pain.
