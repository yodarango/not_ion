1: The moment I become satisfied in my sanctification I will not plateau. I will likely regress. Paul assimilated Christianity to running a race (1 Cor. 9:24; Phil. 3:13; 2 Tim. 4:7). I would like to qualify that the race is not a race on plane ground but a race uphill. The moment I stop running I don’t run the risk of of staying there, but of sliding down to where I started from. It is true that Paul advices to track our progress by comparing our present self to the old one (Gal. 6:4) but even when the outcome results positive, I cannot allow this to fill me with contentment. Paul encourges us here to always pursue more. He advices us to strive to be better. #YLW #DR

3: I must avoid not be concern for the will of God pertaining to my vocation, a sentimental partner, or my place of residence until I have not obeyed His will regarding my lifestyle. I might not have complete assurance of the enterprise I should work for, where I must attend college, or who I must marry primarily because those instructions are not found with explicitness in the Scriptures. However, there are at least four items in the Bible that are unambiguously delivered to me as the will of God:

  1. it is the will of God that everyone is saved (Jn. 6:40). I am sharing the message of the Good News with others? When was the last time I told a coworker, classmate, or family member what God has done for me? When was the last time that I perceived a hurting soul and I was prompted by the Holy Spirit to bring His Word of hope to them? God wills for me to do this.
  1. It is the will of God that I stay away from sin (1 Thes. 4:3). Plain and simple! Until I have not abandoned my old mentality I must not expect God to lead me. The Lord does not drink from dirty cups. If I desire to be sensitive to His voice and directed in matters that are left to my free will to decide, must begin by the sanctification of my life.
  1. It is the will of God that I remain joyful, thankful and constant in your prayer (1 Thes. 5:16-18). The lack of these three are the perfect combination for creating a bitter Christian. If I ever lack these qualities, I should not only question whether my faith is still genuine, but I also must know that until I don’t partake on them, I should not expect to hear from God. How can I expect to be guided by the Good Shepherd when the grass the other sheep are grazing on always looks greener than mine? How can I expect to be delegated a task from my master, when I go through life complaining about His wages for my work? And how can I expect to hear His voice when He never hears mine?
  1. It is the will of God that I eradicate every possibility of defamation against Christianity through my behavior (1 Peter 2:15). I have never been a great admirer of public debates made for spectacle. They might have their benefits, even if I doubt it. They rarely, thought there are exceptions, do anything for the spectator or the opponent. Most people will not be converted to Christianity by shutting down their objections to Christianity through an argument. The most effective form of winning a discussion against an unbeliever is not through an elaborate argument, but through my character. They will not care much about my sophisticated Theology or my eloquent oratory skills on philosophy if my claims do not adhere to my way of life.

In conclusion, until I have not paid close attention to these four explicit wills, I should not expect Him to speak His will to me concerning other areas of life. #DB #DR
