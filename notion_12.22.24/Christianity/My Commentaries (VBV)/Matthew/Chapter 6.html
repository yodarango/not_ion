Chapter 6

1: The verse does not have in context teh general public who is not aware of identity. During this times the pharisees were well known by the people. The knew who they were. Therefore, they were not doing their deeds among a public that did not know who they were. They were doing their deeds among a public that recognized them in order to gain a reputation. Before doing any good deed in or to the public, we should ask ourselves, “would I do the same if nobody was seeing me or even if nobody here knows who I am?” We should follow that question with a second, “Am I being honest with myself?” If the answer is not clear yet, step away from doing it, and your heart will let you know. If your heart feels sorry for those who are being deprived of your help, that might be a good indicator that you indeed care and God’s Spirit is working through you. If you only feel regret that nobody will know what you did, find a way to do it while staying anonymus and ask God to pour of His Spirit on your lack of love. It is important you do not walk away from the need, however. We are called to do what is right. But it is important that we learn how to remove ourselves from the equation. The only instance in which I would advise not to meet the need is if we indeed fail to remove our names from our good works. We might argue that even if we are doing it for vain purposes, helping is still better than not helping. This is true for the recipient, but it will not be true for us. For the giver, a present given from pride will only add to an already inflated ego that will make ur walks with Christ impossible if not controlled (Jam. 4:6). And at the end, what good there is in gaining the world and loosing our souls? (Mat. 16:26) #GR #DR
