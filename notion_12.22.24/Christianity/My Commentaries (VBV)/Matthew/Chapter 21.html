6-9: A story is told about the donkey, who, though being the lowest of all transportation beasts, upon seeing the multitudes surrendering at its entrance thought to himself “My! How important I must be!” Thus, filling itself with pride. How prompt I am to fall in the sin of the donkey. When I attribute the success of my ministry to my own merits I am forgetting that the kingdom is not about myself. I am forgetting that anything that the Lord allows to happen through me does not say anything about a simple man like myself. However, it does say a lot about Him. He who, though being the King, is willing to ride on this simple donkey. May I never forget that if souls are being saved, transformed, and blessed by my ministry, whatever this is, it is not because of what I am, but because of who is directing my steps. #BL #DR

12: God is love, but being love does not equal passivity. Just as water—in its proper amounts—has a positive reaction in living organisms, this is not the case when mixed with acid. Pouring water on a dry on a seed will yield life, but pouring water into a jug of acid will cause an exothermic explosion. God is not less love when He displays His wrath than He displays His blessings. Both reactions are properties of love, and the essence of love never changes. However, its reaction does, and its reaction is determined by that which it gets on His way. If obedience, kindness, compassion, and justice come His way, it will produce life and blessings. If on the other hand, it encounters disobedience, abuse, aggression, and injustice, the reaction will be fatal. Therefore, we must not think of our selves devoid of love when acts of injustice, desecration, irreverence, or the like arise my anger (Act. 13:9-12; 1 Samuel 11:6). Nonetheless, I must not forget I am not God. I do not posess the right to execute justice on my own hand. #BR #DR
