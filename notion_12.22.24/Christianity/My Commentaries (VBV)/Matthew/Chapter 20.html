3-7: This is a beautiful juxtaposition of the saints under the previous covenant against I, a member of His Church. Many are those who have come before myself in order to render me able to do the ministry God has entrusted to me (Jn. 4:37-38). Yet, the reward of enjoying Him forever has been granted to myself as much as it has been to them. Yet, the parable could also symbolize those within the New Covenant today. Not every person of my generation has come to Him at the age of eighteen, as I have. Some of them have come at 20, 25 and yet, some others will come at 30, 50 or perhaps even 60. However, the reward, in terms of quality though not quantity (1 Cor. 3:8), will be the same for all of us. Any servant of Christ that complains about having toiled longer than those who have come later only exposes that he has not enjoyed the labor given to him. If I am a joyful servant, regardless of how difficult the mission assigned to me is and how long I have to work to complete it, I will never be envious of my sibling for being hired. Even if His burden appears lighter, shorter, and more glamorous. Rather, if I truly find satisfaction in serving my Lord, I will be glad and thankful to have given the opportunity to serve Him longer. A joyful servant who is hired at nine never wishes to have been hired at five. A happy servant delights in serving the one who has called Him. Contrary to this, the servant hired at five wishes that he would have made himself available at nine and not have been a procrastinator of five. So reach is this parable of our Lord that there is yet another aspect to be uncovered. That is, that is does not matter how late we arrive to His service, He does not deem our labor cheaper than the labor of those who came before. The labor of those who have come to Him in the last years of their life is just as valuable as mine, who came before. Our senior saint should never be looked on as of less use. What they have to offer to the kingdom of God is as necessary as to what I have to offer. #YLW #DR

7: The men hired at five were not being lazy, they were ready and waiting to be hired. They were prepared to do the job. They did not have to ask for time to go get ready. Had they done so, the opportunity to serve would have escaped by the time the time they were done. If I expect to be of any use to my Lord, I must make myself available and qualified. It is my responsibility to inhale and exhale His Word and to live in constant communion and obedience to Him. It is my responsibility to maintain myself full of His Holy Spirit after He has deposit Him in me (Eph. 5:18). I have heard it say that "God doesn't call the qualified, He qualifies the called.” I believe this agrees with Scripture in terms of sanctification (Rom. 8:30) but not in terms of preparation. He does qualify those whom He calls, but He only send those who have qualified themselves (2 Tim. 2:15). #YLW #DR

22: Any prayer that overrides the desire or will of another believer will most probably receive a “no.” And if it is, it is because God had it planned in that way before in His sovereignty. Asking that two of the disciples sat beside our Lord was asking God to make a choice from favoritism and not on merits. What about the other nine? (leaving out Iscariot sine he wanted no part in this). Surely the other nine wanted to assume that place as well. For this reason they became indignant (verse 24). Christ responds to the woman that this decision rests in the sovereignty of the Father not in the petition of a prayer. Nonetheless, He does give His disciples the key to prominence in His kingdom. They must be servants to the rest. Thus, their reward does not rest in how much they think the Lord likes them, but on the quality of their labor (1 Cor. 3:12-15). I prayed many bold prayers during my single years regarding the finding of my wife. But one principle that I learned from very early in my walk with the Lord was to never elevate a prayer that manipulated the desires of a girl whom I saw potential in. Primarily because I did not want anyone to elevate the same prayer about me. I did not want to be “forced” to like someone. I wanted to like them simply for the nature of their character and the features of their physicality. In other words, I wanted to be attracted to them and I wanted good chemistry between our two personalities. #PNK #DR
