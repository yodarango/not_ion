27: The disciples find themselves overcome by fear, and immediately the Lord speaks to them. We will never encounter a situation in life that God allows us to be overcome by fear. When Abraham feared for the fulfillment of the promise, God was quick to encourage him (Gen. 15:1). When Paul feared for His live, the Lord was quick to reassure him of his safety (Act. 18:91-0). In all of these instances, the Lord addressed the fear of His servants with promptitude. Now, should we expect the Lord to speak to us through a voice in the sky or a vision of the night, or personally present as He did in this examples? Absolutely not. These instances happen during a time when the Holy Spirit was not yet available to the Church. Today we are instructed that God has not given us a Spirit of fear but His Spirit Himself (2 Tim. 1:7). The same Spirit that had Abraham stare at the galaxy, Spoke to Paul, and approached the apostles. As long as we remain filled of His Spirit there will never be a situation in our lives that causes us to give a step back in fear (Psa. 27:1). I do not mean that we will never suffer fear. We will. What I mean is we will never be left locked in a room with it. The Holy Spirit in us, if we have been faithful to cultivate His presence in us, will address the matter as He did in the pas immediately. #PPL #DR
