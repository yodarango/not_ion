1: It is in view of GodÕs mercy that a Christian is en outraged and
enabled to live a holy life. It is not in view of His possible wrath if
we disobey, but in consequence of an unending love and gratitude to the
One who has saved our lives from the craws of satan. A Christian lives a
holy life because he/ she loves God, not because He/ She is afraid of
God. #RD #DR 2: The new birth in Christ is not only a change of heart,
but a change of mind. As a matter of fact, Paul puts the emphasis on the
renewal of our minds as the catalyzer for change, rather than the change
of our heart. For it is impossible to have a change of heart without
first having a change of mind. If we feel that our heart still beats for
the things of this world, we would do good in analyzing our thoughts and
attitude towards the Christ and His word. What do we spend the most time
thinking about, what do we give our sight and hearing to? Because
ultimately those are the main channels through which information to our
brain flows. How do we view the Word of the Lord? Is it another task of
the day that needs to get done or a special and cherished time of our
days?

6: Prophesying, which simply means speaking forth the word of the Lord,
applies to preaching. This is how we can stand protected against any
heresy: by knowing the Word and running every sermon against it to see
whether it passes the test. #BL #DR 6: Although dreams and visions from
God are not something common, and specially not as common as modern
ÒChristianityÓ claims them to be, they do in fact are in exercise today.
But how can we know a dream, vision, or intuition comes from the Lord?
It must be according to the faith. If it does not pass the test of
scripture, it is not from God. #BL 7: I believe God has been given to us
one and only one calling. I do not mean to say that it is not His will
for us to help minister in other areas, but I believe that those are
secondary to our function within the body. Just as each part of the
physical human body performs a multitude of complex functions, it
nevertheless, only serves one principal purpose that encompasses all
others. God has given us one gift and one gift alone because He wants us
to be specialists. He does not want a person that can perform average in
multiple areas but a person that can perform with excellence in the are
that he/ she has been called. Whatever is your calling be faithful to
that. Do not allow your heart and mind to be split by trying to do
everything, give your passion, time, and resources to one that which you
know the Lord has called you to so that you can be faithful to it and do
it to the best of your ability for your good Master.

9: Paul gives the formula for sincere love: to hate what is evil and to
love what is good. True love will lead us to detest anything that
dissolves it. It is not good enough for a lover of Christ to stay away
from what it is evil, but to hate it; to hate it because it hurts the
One we love; to hate it because it intrudes and damages the relationship
with the One our heart beats for. If you are in Christ but can still
think of your sinful past with a sense of pleasure, check your heart.
Make sure you have truly repented. It is interesting, however, that Paul
does not use what we would consider the main antonym of hate: love, when
he goes on to restate his idea in the last pet of the verse. Instead of
saying Òlove what is goodÓ he says Òkollomenoi what is good;Ó he employs
the word Òkolla,Ó the term where we get our cola word from. He is
telling us to adhere ourselves to what is good. In other words, as far
as is within your ability, watch only what it good. Listen only to what
is good. Form close relationships only to those who have the same
mindset of doing good. We must Surround ourselves with that which is
good [2 Timothy 2:22].

11: It is impossible to maintain a real zeal for God while being lazy in
our mini is try and slacking in our spiritual life. If we donÕt feel the
fervor that we use to at one time, we must stop and reflect, what have
we stop doing. We will only maintain and passion for God and for that
which He loves by serving Him. #GR #DR 16: the word use for those of
Òlow positionÓ here is the word Òtapeinos.Ó From this word the Latin
derived its word tapetis, which eventually became the Spanish word for
Òtapete,Ó meaning doormat in English. Therefore, Paul is not only asking
us to be willing to associate ourselves with those who are not in the
same socioeconomic class as we are, or those who are not as educated as
we are, or those who have a past that has tarnished their moral
reputation, but he is referring to those who according to the world, are
the lowest of humanity. We must remember that not matter how poor or how
limited the education, or how bad the past of our brothers and sister in

sexes, or races [Colossians 3:28]. We are heirs with Christ of our
FatherÕs blessings. It is disgusting to see Christians make little
clicks in the church that no one outside their approval can seem to

17: when you take revenge on your own hands you are taking the place of

Revenge is His, and I have lived long enough to know that things do not
go well when you steal from Him. Why bring the consequences of this evil
