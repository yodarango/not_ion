Chapter 3

3: Three characteristics of a true believer. 1. Serve God genuinely. 2. Are not ashamed to stand for what's right. 3. Are not attached to this world.

4: It is hard to trust in God when things are going down hill in your life. The you get fired from that job, when she or he only wants to be friends, when you suffer betrayal, when the Doctor's report is not so good, when you are in the middle of a dessert alone and without anyone to extend hand to you. But have you tried trusting in God when everything is going just right in life? When there is abundance, health stability, a great family, and goals coming to fulfillment? That, right there, is the real test. God did not warn His people about forgetting Him in the lack but about forgetting Him in the plenty [Deuteronomy 8:14]. Therefore, this is a crucial spiritual key that Paul had. To be able to trust the Lord even when he could on His flesh. Can you, in spite of what seat you occupy in life's Ferry's Wheel, trust Him today?

5: Benjamin was the leading tribe. The motives for Paul’s boast about his tribe. [psalm 68:27]

10: The word used here is (Dunamis) It is tragic to see in our day preachers who know Christ, but they are as far from knowing the power of His resurrection as someone could be. Yes they know the history of Jesus. the enjoy of a rich education and theological knowledge. They can quote the bible from cover to cover. But oh, when it comes about believing in miracles and divine intervention -unless it is for judgment- how they struggle. That Is why you should not stop with knowing Christ, but experience His Holy Spirit.

8: If, after coming to Christ, we live with the mindset that we are losing out on the things of the world rather than winning in Christ, we will not last long. If we do not halt this attitude on time, eventually the struggles of life itself and the difficulties and sacrifices that imply being a Christian will not make sense anymore. We will arrive to a place where we feel that our investment is lower than the profit. We can correct this if we learn, with the help of the Holy Spirit, to relabel what we consider an investment and what we consider to be a profit. However, we must know that, at the end, we will always end up walking the path our heart has laid for us. Whether we choose salvation for our love to Christ, or perdition for the love to sin (Matt. 6:21). #BR #DR

12: It says Christ took hold of you, so that you can take hold of the prize, which He has already won for you too, by the way. Albeit Christ has already wont this price for you He expects you to run for it. Although it is faith that saves He expects works. In other words, taking the parable in Luke 13, He expects you to bear fruit. He planted planted you (won you/ took hold of you) so that You could bear fruit (take hold of the prize)

13: Being able to forget was the remarkable secret for Paul's clear conscience [1 Corinthians 4:4] and a powerful weapon in his armament against the devil. He gave no chance to satan to instill discouragement from past mistakes. Listen, Im going to be very lucid here. I don't care if you have raped, murdered, stolen, betrayed, abandoned, adulterer, fornicated, or blasphemed God. Yes, I know all of those are horrible, nasty actions. Yes I am aware they might be as recent as yesterday, but if you really love Christ. If you really have a heart to follow He who gave His live for you, and if you have genuinely repented, forget those things and move on! If God is not stuck looking at them why are you? If God has already threw them into the sea why are you still carry them? God has called you to carry a cross not your sins. He did that.

15: If you thin differently He will show you how to think. What a beautiful thing! Paul is talking about putting not confidence in the flesh but in Christ. He is speaking about leaving everything behind to put Christ first. Perhaps you might be a very successful business person, renown doctor, prominent personality, Hollywood star, eloquent politician, or remarkable athlete. Perhaps you are not any of this, but are in your way there. Perhaps this is the engine in your life, the reason why you wake up every morning. The dreams that you have been chasing for so long. Or perhaps you are new born Christian, you have walk this way for quite some time now when all of a sudden God calls you to abandon those things. To put them behind and don't look back. I know it makes no sense and you can reason it out, but if you just hold on and pray wholeheartedly He wont fail to make it clear to you that whatever plans He has for you, are best. For not only does He teach you to think, He gives you the desire to do it, as well as the strength required to do it [Philippians 2:13]

16: See, God will not demand from you that which you are not aware of. You are responsible for the amount of light given to you.

17: If we are to put our eyes n someone it should be those who are following the example of Christ. We often keep an eye in those who are living the lowest levels of the Christian life. No consecration, no intentional prayer and dedicated fasting; Not sold out to the word simply to make us feel better about ourselves. Paul is saying, look for those who best follow the steps of Christ around you and mimic them, because one day, or perhaps even now, you have people patterning their lives after yours and take you as a standard. Is you're choice, you can either be the Lowest-Acceptable-Level-of-Mediocrity and have people compare to you when they want to fell better about themselves, or the be the Highest-Respectable-Level-of-Accuracy that they look up to when they want to improve themselves.
