1: If Christ indeed has possession of the greatest power in heaven, what is the Father’s place? Or why would the Father even presume of a throne when He has no power? This is the proof of the absolute uniqueness of the ONE God. #PPL #DR #ONE

2: At the time Paul wrote this, John had not yet written down his revelation of heaven and, Paul, the only other apostle to whom it was revealed, could not speak about it (2 Cor. 12:2-4). What were the Colossians to understand by “the things of heaven”? The most sound answer is anything related to the kingdom of God (Phil. 4:8). That which is eternal good for the soul and not temporarily pleasant to the body (2 Cor. 4:18). In a more poetic statement, I could say that God calls me to have my head in the clouds and this is the reason the world calls me crazy and stupid. This is opposite to the thought of the age, which claims that anything that is not natural is not true. Since God is not natural, spending a life meditating and pleasing Him, will never be understood by the world. Nonetheless, I rather pass on this earth as a crazy man with his head on the clouds than as an arrogant being who attempts to get the heavens into his head. I could not say it better than G. K. Chesterton in his work, Orthodoxy, "the poet only asks to get his head into the heavens. It is the logician that seeks to get the heavens into his head and hence cracks." #GR #DR

5: The greek νεκρώσατε, which is delivered in the english as “put to death,” is an active verb in its imperative form. Meaning that I am commanded to execute this order. It is my responsibility to kill the things being listed. The fact that the Holy Spirit renders available to me everything that I need eradicate (2 Tim. 1:7) them does not mean He will do it for me. R. L. Keiper puts in the following way “…we have been given everything we need to live for Christ. But we must do the living.” It is the watcher that needs to kill watching pornography, he fornicator and adulterer who needs to stop these dirty acts, the gossiper who needs to stop back-bitting other believers, and the thief that needs to stop making money unjustly. #BR #DR

8: On the day I am writing this I came across a social media page with the username “@christianswhocursesometimes” (christians who curse sometimes). Sadly but not surprisingly, the page has 578K followers as of today. This is exactly what Paul is warning us against. As a Christian I am called to have not only a clean attitude but a clean mouth. The mouth is a window into the heart (Lk. 6:45). When someone opts for obscene words or dirty jokes, they are only stating that they refuse to let the old man go and that they have not allowed the Holy Spirit to work freely in their heart. There is bitter waters of corruption that do not allow the sweet water of Life to flow (Jas. 3:10-12). Christians who willingly curse sometimes are Christians who quite probably live in the flesh most of the time. #BR #DR

10: The objective of a Christian is to become as perfect as Christ. That is our challenge. Nevertheless, here we are told that we ought to be as our creator. Therefore Christ is our creator. And if Christ our Creator [Colossians 1:16] than there's none but ONE God! #PPL #DR

You have passed from being a slave of fear to being a slave of peace! How beautiful my friend! #RD #DR
